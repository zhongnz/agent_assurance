## Maintainer responsibilities

- **Repository hygiene.** Maintaining the version history, the CHANGELOG, the release-checklist discipline, branch protection, and citation metadata accuracy.
- **Substantive engagement.** Responding to Issues and pull requests on a rolling basis; engaging the substance of submitted critiques, corrections, and proposals against the standard articulated in `CONTRIBUTING.md`.
- **Release cadence.** Cutting tagged releases per `RELEASE_CHECKLIST.md`. Updating CITATION.cff and Zenodo metadata after each release.
- **Methodology stewardship.** Maintaining alignment between the architectural argument (paper), the operational specification (control matrix), the applied demonstrations (case study and reference application), and the operational layer (assurance kit, MVA).
- **Conduct.** Holding the line on the `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md` expectations within project spaces.

## Becoming a maintainer

There is no formal process at present. Substantive contributors who engage the methodology over time may be invited to co-maintain as the work compounds. The criteria, when they're operationalised, would centre on:

- **Methodology depth.** Demonstrated understanding of the architectural argument and the design principles, with engagement that improves the methodology rather than merely extending its surface.
- **Engagement experience.** Application of the methodology to live deployments (with institutional consent for what is shared) — the kind of empirical grounding the public-record-only development cannot produce.
- **Trust over time.** Sustained, constructive engagement with the project across multiple revisions, demonstrating the kind of judgement co-maintenance requires.

If the project's engagement scale reaches a point where co-maintenance is needed, this section will be updated to reflect the process and the criteria.
